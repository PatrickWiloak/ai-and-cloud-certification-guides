# FinOps Certified Practitioner

## Exam Overview

The FinOps Certified Practitioner (FOCP) exam validates your understanding of cloud financial management principles and practices. This certification demonstrates your ability to apply the FinOps framework to manage cloud costs effectively, communicate cost information across an organization, and drive financial accountability in cloud usage.

**Exam Details:**
- **Certification Body:** FinOps Foundation (part of The Linux Foundation)
- **Duration:** 60 minutes
- **Number of Questions:** 50 multiple choice questions
- **Passing Score:** 70%
- **Cost:** $300 USD
- **Delivery:** Online proctored
- **Prerequisites:** None (cloud experience recommended)
- **Validity:** 2 years
- **Recertification:** Retake the exam or earn continuing education credits

## Exam Domains

### Domain 1: Understanding FinOps (20%)
- Define FinOps and its value proposition
- Explain the FinOps lifecycle (Inform, Optimize, Operate)
- Describe the six FinOps principles
- Identify FinOps stakeholders and their roles
- Understand the FinOps maturity model (Crawl, Walk, Run)

**Key Concepts:**
- FinOps is a cultural practice, not just tooling
- Cross-functional collaboration between finance, engineering, and business
- Cloud cost management as a shared responsibility
- Iterative approach to financial optimization
- FinOps team structure and responsibilities

### Domain 2: Inform Phase (30%)
- Implement cost allocation and tagging strategies
- Create showback and chargeback models
- Build cost visibility through dashboards and reports
- Understand cloud billing models and pricing structures
- Identify cost anomalies and trending

**Key Concepts:**
- Tagging strategies and governance
- Cost allocation methods (direct, shared, amortized)
- Cloud provider billing structures (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- Budgeting and forecasting techniques
- Cost reporting best practices
- Unit economics and metrics

### Domain 3: Optimize Phase (25%)
- Apply rate optimization strategies
- Implement usage optimization techniques
- Evaluate commitment-based discounts
- Identify and eliminate waste
- Right-size cloud resources

**Key Concepts:**
- Reserved Instances and Savings Plans
- Spot/preemptible instances
- Right-sizing recommendations
- Idle resource identification
- Storage optimization
- Licensing optimization
- Architectural optimization

### Domain 4: Operate Phase (25%)
- Establish FinOps governance processes
- Create policies and guardrails
- Drive organizational adoption
- Implement continuous improvement
- Align cloud spending with business value

**Key Concepts:**
- Policy enforcement and compliance
- Automation of cost management tasks
- KPIs and metrics for FinOps success
- Organizational change management
- Vendor management and negotiation
- Cloud cost optimization lifecycle

## Study Approach

### Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2)
1. **FinOps Framework**
   - Read the FinOps Framework documentation thoroughly
   - Understand the six principles of FinOps
   - Learn the three phases (Inform, Optimize, Operate)
   - Study the FinOps maturity model

2. **Cloud Billing Basics**
   - Understand how cloud providers charge
   - Learn about different pricing models
   - Study billing concepts across AWS, Azure, and GCP

### Phase 2: Deep Dive (Week 3-4)
1. **Cost Allocation and Visibility**
   - Master tagging strategies
   - Understand showback vs chargeback
   - Study cost allocation methods
   - Learn about budgeting and forecasting

2. **Optimization Strategies**
   - Rate optimization (RIs, Savings Plans, spot)
   - Usage optimization (rightsizing, waste reduction)
   - Architectural optimization patterns

### Phase 3: Exam Preparation (Week 5-6)
1. **Practice and Review**
   - Take practice exams
   - Review the FinOps Framework one more time
   - Focus on weak areas
   - Study real-world scenarios

2. **Operational Excellence**
   - Governance and policy creation
   - Organizational alignment
   - Continuous improvement processes

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Showback** | Reporting costs to teams without actual billing |
| **Chargeback** | Billing costs directly to responsible teams |
| **Right-sizing** | Matching resource capacity to actual workload needs |
| **Unit Economics** | Cost per unit of business value (cost per customer, transaction, etc.) |
| **Commitment-based Discounts** | RIs, Savings Plans, CUDs that exchange commitment for lower rates |
| **Amortization** | Spreading upfront costs over the commitment period |
| **Blended Rate** | Average rate across on-demand and discounted usage |
| **Unblended Rate** | Actual rate charged for each individual resource |
| **Waste** | Resources provisioned but not used or underutilized |
| **Tagging** | Applying metadata labels to cloud resources for cost allocation |

## Common Exam Patterns

1. **Framework Questions**: Expect questions on FinOps principles, lifecycle phases, and maturity model
2. **Stakeholder Identification**: Know which personas are involved in FinOps and their responsibilities
3. **Optimization Priority**: Understand when to optimize rate vs usage vs architecture
4. **Best Practices**: Questions on tagging, showback/chargeback, and governance
5. **Scenario-Based**: Real-world situations requiring FinOps recommendations
6. **Cloud-Agnostic**: The exam is not specific to one cloud provider
7. **Cultural Questions**: FinOps as a practice emphasizing collaboration and culture change
